COUSCOUS

description:

couscous is a Algerien dish of small steamed granules of rolled durum wheat semolina that is traditionally served with a stew spooned on top

ingredients:

  • 1-3/4 cups low sodium chicken or vegetable broth (or water)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1-1/2 cups (10 oz) couscous

steps:

  1. To start, bring the cooking liquid (preferably a flavorful chicken or vegetable broth) to a boil in a medium pot. Add a drizzle of olive oil, a pad of butter, and a little salt.
  2. Next, add the couscous. (I use 1-3/4 cups liquid to 1-1/2 cups couscous.)
  3. Take the pan off the heat, cover, and let the couscous steam for 5 minutes.
  4. When you lift the lid, the grains will appear flat in an even layer. Use a fork to fluff it up and break up the clumps for light and fluffy couscous
